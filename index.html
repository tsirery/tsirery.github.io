About Me

Tsirery Christian Randriamiarisoa holds a PhD in Philosophy from the Université d'Antananarivo, where his research focused on epistemology and the theory of representation. His doctoral thesis, Essai sur les conditions de possibilité de la représentation à l'ère de la post-vérité, develops the concept of inter-représentation — arguing that under post-truth conditions, the subject's role in representation must expand into intersubjectivity — built around a triadic framework of truth, rationality, and objectivity.

He currently teaches logic and epistemology at the Mention Philosophie (Université d'Antananarivo) and is pursuing a second degree in Economics at CNTEMAD, alongside an affiliation with the Centre de Bioéthique et d'Éthique Appliquée (CBEA). This engagement reflects a broader commitment to applied ethics, which he also seeks to instill in his students by encouraging them toward applied philosophy rather than purely theoretical inquiry.

Civic Engagement & Philosophy

His concern for applied ethics extends beyond the classroom: he is involved with the association Tsiok'Aina Maitso ("Green Breath"), which raises public awareness in urban areas about the health and long-term risks of air pollution, reflecting a deeper commitment to sustainable urban living.

Across these domains — theoretical, pedagogical, and civic — his work is guided by a single conviction: that questions of truth and rationality are not confined to the seminar room, but bear directly on how we reason, act, and live together.
